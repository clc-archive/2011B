Capital Region Economic Development Council

November 10, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Working Farms | Keywords: Capital Region Economic Development Council

Support Working Farms in the Capital Region and Columbia County

Right now, NY State is developing a new economic strategy and a new funding system to support that strategy. CLC wants to make sure that economic strategy focuses on working farms. As of 2007, there were 554 farms in Columbia County which generated $66,000,000 in annual revenue, and demand for local food continues to increase. Compared to industrial and residential land, working farms generate more revenue and require less public services, saving everyone on taxes.

NY State is dividing the state into economic zones, Columbia County is in the ‘Capital Region’, which stretches from Greene and Columbia to Washington County. State funding will be distributed to each zone and apportioned based on our region’s official economic plan developed by the Capital Region Economic Development Council. The Council is developing the plan now and is taking comments from the public.

Great Song Farm

November 9, 2011 | Posted in CLC, Farmer Landowner Match Program, Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: climate change, csa, farm, great song, hawthorne valley, photos

In 2010, the US imported 164.4 million pounds of “fresh garlic” and garlic imports continue to rise. However, in 2012 there will be a hundred or so people in the Hudson Valley who will be getting fresh local garlic from Great Song Farm in Milan, NY. This fall, Great Song is completing its first growing season and is planting a plot of garlic to be harvested next year.

The farmers at Great Song don’t own the land where they are planting their garlic and their other vegetables. They obtained the use of the property through CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program. CONTINUE READING

November 3, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Ag Funding, Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: land trust alliance, purchase of development rights

Logs on Berkshire Farm property

The Senate and House Agriculture Committees will be making decisions that could dramatically effect the amount of federal conservation funding available to family farmers and organizations.

We need your help in urging the Agriculture Committees to support these conservation programs when they make their recommendations on the 2012 Farm Bill to the Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction.
