Informational Session on the Grassland Reserve Program

June 17, 2011 | Posted in Non CLC Event, Working Farms | Keywords: grassland

Webinar on Tuesday, June 21
4:00 PM – 5:00 PM EDT

The Partnership of Rangeland Trusts (PORT) and the Farmland Information Center will co-host via a webinar an informational session and Q + A with Liz Crane, Acting FRPP/HFRP Program Manager, Barbara Eggers, Acting GRP Program Manager, and Mike Beam, Executive Director, Ranchland Trust of Kansas, on the GRP Final Rule, program highlights and current status.

PORT is an alliance of state-wide ranching land trusts in California, Colorado, Kansas, Montana, Oregon, Texas and Wyoming.  PORT was formed to increase the impact that its members could have on national land conservation policy, represent the needs of working rangelands within America’s broader land trust community, and enhance the sharing of information and expertise.  PORT member land trusts collectively hold and administer over 1,100 conservation easements on more than 1.7 million acres.

The Farmland Information Center is a clearinghouse for information about farmland protection and stewardship.  It is a partnership between the USDA Natural Resources Conservation Service and American Farmland Trust.
