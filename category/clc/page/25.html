Siegel-Kline Kill Stocked With Trout

May 2, 2011 | Posted in Arts & Entertainment, CLC, Headline


Grab your fishing polls and head over to Siegel-Kline Kill Conservation Area. Working with the Columbia Land Conservancy and Trout Unlimited, the New York Department of Environmental Conservation (NYSDEC) introduced fifty brown trout into the creek at the public conservation area. Fishing licenses can be obtained at most town clerk offices and sporting good stores.

Brewster Pettus Joins CLC Board

April 26, 2011 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: board

Pettus was active in CLC’s acquisition of Siegel-Kline Kill Conservation Area

The Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) welcomes Brewster Pettus to the board of trustees. Pettus and his family have had a home in Columbia County for more than 25 years, and have a long-standing commitment to conservation in the county.

Pettus has a law degree from Fordham University. In addition to practicing law, he has worked for several non-profit organizations and the New York State Department of State Division of Coastal Resources, and has owned and operated a retail business.

As a regular supporter of the Columbia Land Conservancy, I have long admired their tenacious and effective efforts to protect the natural beauty and rural character of Columbia County. As a new board member, I am thankful for the opportunity to help preserve this beautiful part of the world. A place that my mother fell in love with in 1982, I’ve enjoyed for most of my life, and that I hope my daughters will also love and appreciate for many years to come.

Brewster Pettus
