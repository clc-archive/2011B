82-Acre Farm Protected, Additional 84 Acres put into Conservation

September 15, 2011 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: csa, easements, purchase of development rights, stuyvesant

Monkshood Barn

David Rowley working in greenhouse

We believe Columbia County is singularly well positioned to take advantage of the surge of interest in healthy and locally grown food, and that a resurgent and viable farm economy would, among other things, be an excellent conservation achievement. Scenic Hudson has been an invaluable partner in furthering this vision.

CLC Executive Director Peter Paden

Scenic Hudson and the Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) have partnered to purchase development rights on an 82-acre farm in the Town of Stuyvesant, guaranteeing that it will continue contributing to the community’s agriculture-based economy and rural charm. By protecting the farm, which contains 57 acres of USDA Prime Soils and Soils of Statewide Significance, the organizations have made it economically feasible for the land’s former leaseholder, Monkshood Nursery, to purchase the property. Photo Show – Call for Entries

September 6, 2011 | Posted in CLC | Keywords: photos

Photography in the Landscape, Celebrating 25 Years of Conservation

The Columbia Land Conservancy announces a juried photography exhibition that calls attention to the natural and agricultural beauty of Columbia County. Professionals and amateurs, as well as youth, are asked to submit photographs by Friday, October 7, 2011. The exhibit is juried by Carrie Haddad, who owns and manages two galleries in Hudson – Carrie Haddad Gallery and Carrie Haddad Photographs. The exhibit opens at Spencertown Academy on Saturday, October 15.

Columbia County is a place of extraordinary and incredibly varied beauty. We’re asking photographers to document the wonders of the land we inhabit and to show us new ways of looking at it. Works could include a broad range of subjects from panoramic landscapes to close-ups of agricultural life or the smallest flower. This exhibit will inspire people to appreciate our natural world and build support for conserving it.

Monarch Butterfly

August 25, 2011 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Nature | Keywords: Education Resources, greenport, knee-high, monarch, schor

Center: Magnified image of caterpillar on a leaf. Right: Young monarch caterpillar, measures 2.5mm

2 weeks later, caterpillar has grown bigger

LIFE OF THE MONARCH

When it emerges from it’s egg, it is a caterpillar less than a millimeter long. It munches on milkweed and within a week it grows 100 times in size. By the second week, it leaves the milkweed and heads to higher ground. It hangs in a “J” shape and shortens in length. It’s body transforms into a smooth cocoon called a chrysalis. Then 2 weeks later, it emerges a Monarch butterfly.

There are official Monarch butterfly preserves called Monarch Waystations throughout the Americas, including Schor and Greenport Conservation Areas. These areas provide resources necessary for Monarchs to produce successive generations and sustain their migration, such as milkweed and nectar sources.

Geology of Greenport

August 18, 2011 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Nature | Keywords: geology, greenport

Entrance of the conservation area used to be the shore of Glacial Lake Albany

Around 11,500 BCE, melting glaciers combined with waters from the Mohawk river to form Glacial Lake Albany. Lake Albany ran 100 miles north/south. It was 10 miles across and the shore came to the edge of the Greenport Conservation Area parking lot.

The Access-for-All trail takes visitors along what used to be the floor of the lake. The trail gradually descends to where the deeper waters once stood. The lake-bottom was primarily composed of silt and clay and due to the nature of the lake had very few large rocks. The ground hasn’t changed much and the soil on either side of the Access-For-All-Trail is still representative of what the lake floor once was.
