CLC Expands Support for County-Wide Computer-Aided Map Uses

November 13, 2012 | Posted in Community Assistance, Headline | Keywords: gis

CLC is training municipal volunteers to utilize computer-generated maps in their planning and land use decisions. Last spring, more than three dozen volunteers selected by their town boards took part in trainings using simplified versions of Geographic Information Systems (GIS) software. They also received a data disk with extensive mapping of Columbia County’s infrastructure and natural resources which can now be used by those towns.

This training program, funded by the Hudson River Greenway, will be expanded in its second year with help from a Hudson River Estuary Program “mini-grant.” The additional funding allows CLC to partner again with Dutchess County Cornell Cooperative Extension’s GIS Resource Educator to offer the training. It also allows CLC to add a new workshop component: Nan Stolzenburg and Don Meltz of Community Planning and Environmental Associates will offer two workshops on the new Environmental Assessment Forms issued by NYSDEC that become mandatory for SEQR reviews on April 1, 2013. These workshops will also include information on GIS-based information resources to help in completing the forms. CONTINUE READING

Aerial Monitoring 2012

November 9, 2012 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: aerial, easements, photos

It’s a bird, it’s a plane, no it’s CLC in a plane. Once a year, CLC takes to the sky in order to monitor all of the lands on which we hold conservation easements. The aerial perspective is breathtaking. The county is split into vast green meadows and snow covered woodland slopes. The landscape is dotted with ponds, rolling hills, and working farms. These are all the features you see on the ground, only from this unique perspective you see how they are interconnected. From the skies there is a surprising amount of water, houses tucked away from the main roads, and a diversity of landscapes. CONTINUE READING

Visit The Keep Conservation Preserve

November 6, 2012 | Posted in CLC, Community Assistance, Headline, Trails

Visit the recently improved Keep Conservation Preserve in Germantown, NY. It’s 143 acres of woods, wetlands, and old farm fields. Mowed paths and wooded trails provide an easy walk where many species of breeding, nesting, and resident birds can be see throughout the year. CLC assisted the Keep Conservation Foundation with installation of trail signage, a kiosk and trail map, and facilitated the design and printing of a preserve brochure.

Participate in Survey

October 23, 2012 | Posted in CLC, Headline

Participate In Survey

The Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) is asking the public to participate in a short, web-based survey about conservation priorities in Columbia County today. The survey results will be used to help update CLC’s strategic plan, which will outline specific objectives CLC will pursue to carry out its mission over the next five years and examine the financial and human resources needed to accomplish those objectives.

CLC believes strongly that its conservation efforts should reflect the community’s vision. Having broad-based participation in this process is extremely important. We will very much appreciate the input we receive in this survey. We hope for and will welcome participation by many people, representing a cross-section of views.

CLC’s Executive Director, Peter Paden

Pond Returns

October 15, 2012 | Posted in Headline | Keywords: beaver, hand hollow, pca

In late August, we reported that one of the ponds at Hand Hollow Conservation Area had disappeared. A human-made concrete dam, built in the 1950’s, stopped functioning and the water drained. Neighbors expressed their regrets and the beavers listened. Beavers, active in the area, have plugged the breach and the pond has refilled. Beavers create ponds for protection using the waterways as safe passage between their home and food sources.
