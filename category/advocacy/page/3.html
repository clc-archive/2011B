Capital Region Economic Development Council

November 10, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Working Farms | Keywords: Capital Region Economic Development Council

Support Working Farms in the Capital Region and Columbia County

Right now, NY State is developing a new economic strategy and a new funding system to support that strategy. CLC wants to make sure that economic strategy focuses on working farms. As of 2007, there were 554 farms in Columbia County which generated $66,000,000 in annual revenue, and demand for local food continues to increase. Compared to industrial and residential land, working farms generate more revenue and require less public services, saving everyone on taxes.

NY State is dividing the state into economic zones, Columbia County is in the ‘Capital Region’, which stretches from Greene and Columbia to Washington County. State funding will be distributed to each zone and apportioned based on our region’s official economic plan developed by the Capital Region Economic Development Council. The Council is developing the plan now and is taking comments from the public.

Tax Incentives for Conservation – Real and Imagined

June 10, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Headline, Land Matters, Publication | Keywords: easements, Gibson, Gillibrand, Schumer, tax incentive

Part 1 of 2
Originally Printed in the Register-Star

by Peter Paden
May 27, 2011

I just received word that a bill has been introduced in the House of Representatives in Washington D.C. that would significantly enhance federal income tax incentives for landowners of moderate financial means to conserve their property. These days it is not uncommon for people to own property that has increased in value far out of proportion to their overall financial resources. They may love their land and want to protect it with a conservation easement, which would permanently restrict its development potential. But the tax incentives have been structured in such a way that many property owners do not have enough annual income to realize a meaningful financial benefit. They have quite legitimately felt that they cannot afford to conserve their land. This bill would go a long way to correct that inequity and would provide a substantial boost to conservation. Enhanced Easement Incentive

May 20, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Headline | Keywords: enhanced easement incentive

Congressman Chris Gibson, who represents Columbia County, recently signed on as a co-sponsor of the Enhanced Easement Incentive. This legislation, which is expected to be introduced in the House in a matter of days, would allow property owners of moderate financial means to take much fuller advantage of the financial incentives for donating a conservation easement. It creates particularly strong incentives to farmers and ranchers. The bill now has 244 co-sponsors. NY Senator Kirsten Gillibrand is one of ten Senators who have co-sponsored a similar bill in the Senate called the Rural Heritage Conservation Extension Act (S. 339). Be sure to thank our representatives who have signed on to support local conservation, and encourage those who haven’t yet to do so.

Senator Schumer sponsors Public Good IRA Rollover Act

April 26, 2011 | Posted in Advocacy, Headline

Senator Chuck Schumer (D-NY) has introduced the Public Good IRA Rollover Act of 2011 (S. 557). The legislation would expand and make permanent the IRA charitable rollover, lifting the $100,000 charitable gift limit and allowing for certain giving, like to a charitable remainder trust, to begin as early as age 59 ½. At this point, the legislation has 9 additional co-sponsors.
