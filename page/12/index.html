Students Remove Invasive Species

June 12, 2012

For two days, students from Hudson High School’s Environmental Club measured out botanical plots to identify and map the different types and quantity of invasive plant species near trails at the Greenport Conservation Area. They were comparing the quantity, size, and diversity of invasive plants between a new trail and an old trail to see what the impact was of opening an undisturbed area to human usage. They then properly removed (by hand) all of the invasives in their plots. Each day ended with a competition to see which team could hand pull the most Garlic Mustard from designated sites on the yellow trail. The students were completely engaged and enthusiastic – they removed 38 bags (45 gallons each) on the first day!

CLC Supports County-Wide Computer-Aided Mapping

June 5, 2012

Working with the Columbia County Environmental Management Council and with the endorsement of the Columbia County Board of Supervisors, CLC recently obtained funding for the county to train municipalities to utilize Geographic Information Systems (GIS), a computer-aided mapping technology. With this technology, municipalities have the information and infrastructure to make strategic conservation and land use decisions.

The program provides GIS software, data, and training to volunteers selected by their municipalities. So far, Neil Curri, GIS Resource Educator with Cornell Cooperative Extension Dutchess County (CCEDC) has trained 29 volunteers from 13 towns to use the software. A second round of training for additional volunteers will be scheduled later this year. CLC is coordinating the program. CONTINUE READING

Photography in the Landscape Audience Award

May 31, 2012

Congratulations to David Noel Edwards! His photograph “Beebe Pond, Canaan” has won the Columbia Land Conservancy Photography in the Landscape Audience Award. Visitors to the online gallery cast over 80,000 votes and chose Edwards’s photo as their favorite of over 100 entries.

CSArt

May 17, 2012

Hay Bale by Angela Manno

Internationally exhibited artist Angela Manno will be painting the fertile farmlands, historic barns, grazing sheep, and bucolic pastures throughout Columbia County and the iconic Hudson Valley this summer. Art-lovers and supporters of the Columbia Land Conservancy can acquire Manno’s award-winning pastel landscapes at considerable savings, through participating in her innovative Community Supported Art (CSArt) program. Inspired by Community Supported Agriculture, instead of receiving a season’s worth of fresh healthy produce, CSArt participants get a beautiful original pastel of the local landscape – artwork that is food for the soul.

The artist will donate to the Columbia Land Conservancy 10% of the proceeds from each painting that is purchased through this program. Manno also donated to the Country Barbecue Auction.

Manno’s work has been exhibited at the National Museum of Women in the Arts in Washington, D.C., is in the permanent fine art collection of the Smithsonian Institution, and is in private collections throughout the world. The opportunity to reserve a painting through this program ends June 15, 2012.

Kids and Alpacas

May 10, 2012

This week, CLC sponsored a farm visit for a local Head Start group to tour Spruce Ridge Farm. The 3 and 4 year olds were able to feed alpacas and a llama. Plus, they saw cows, guinea hens, and chickens. They learned all about how alpaca fiber (fur) is made into yarn and used to make sweaters, hats, mittens, and scarves. Thanks to Spruce Ridge Farm owners, Jeff and Steve, for being such wonderful hosts!
