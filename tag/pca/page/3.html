CLC Volunteer Receives Award

April 5, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: greenport, pca, volunteer

Arlene Brown with Bernadette Orr cleaning bluebird boxes

Congratulations to CLC Volunteer Arlene Brown! She was named as a “Senior Volunteer of the Year” by the Columbia County Office of the Aging. As a CLC trail monitor at the Greenport Conservation Area for many years, Arlene was responsible for cleaning out more than 20 bluebird boxes every spring to get them ready for the new arrivals. Arlene also helped to ensure the Public Conservation Area is accessible and attractive by clearing trails and notifying CLC of any problems. She also volunteers at the Hudson Area Library.

I feel quite honored. I have always enjoyed doing my activities and meeting people at the Columbia Land Conservancy.

Arlene Brown

Volunteers like Arlene are an integral part of CLC’s ability to conserve rural landscapes and connect people to the land. Volunteers help build trails, represent CLC at community events, contribute to CLC education programs, assist with CLC events, and complete office tasks.

“It’s great that the Office of the Aging is recognizing Arlene’s contribution to CLC and to the people of Columbia County. We are all thankful for her dedication.” –Marcia Cary, CLC’s Membership and Volunteer Coordinator

Sign up to become a CLC volunteer by using our online form or contact Nate Davis at 518.392.5252, ext. 205 or nated@clctrust.org.

Round Ball Mountain Expansion

January 11, 2013 | Posted in Headline, Trails | Keywords: pca, Round Ball Mountain

Outdoor enthusiasts will soon have a new place to visit – the expanded Round Ball Mountain Conservation Area.The Columbia Land Conservancy owns and manages the Public Conservation Area and is adding 310 acres as well as a significant trail extension to the site.

Site improvements and trail construction will get underway this year. The expanded portion will include a trail that will lead from the woods of the existing conservation area, across a ravine before climbing along a ridge known locally as “Fox Hill”, providing expansive views of the surrounding mountains and valley. Pond Returns

October 15, 2012 | Posted in Headline | Keywords: beaver, hand hollow, pca

In late August, we reported that one of the ponds at Hand Hollow Conservation Area had disappeared. A human-made concrete dam, built in the 1950’s, stopped functioning and the water drained. Neighbors expressed their regrets and the beavers listened. Beavers, active in the area, have plugged the breach and the pond has refilled. Beavers create ponds for protection using the waterways as safe passage between their home and food sources.

New Greenport Entrance

September 18, 2012 | Posted in CLC | Keywords: greenport, pca

CLC is constructing a new entrance and parking area at the Greenport Conservation Area. The new entrance will be located on Joslen Blvd., a few hundred yards north of the current main entrance; a new trail will connect the new parking area to the existing trail system. The new parking area will provide better public access to the site and reduce maintenance expenses. For safety reasons, visitors should not enter the construction area, including the new connector trail. CLC will announce when the new entrance and trail are open to the public.
