Volunteers Improve Schor

July 17, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: pca, schor, volunteer

Volunteers recently joined Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) staff at the Schor Conservation Area for a productive trail day. At the summit, we thinned vegetation, revealing spectacular views. We also cleared out hanging vines and plants encroaching on the trails around Jon’s Pond. The Schor Conservation Area is one of 10 Public Conservation Areas that CLC manages. Volunteers provide crucial support for trail maintenance and site improvements.

Bat Boxes

November 4, 2011 | Posted in Headline, Nature | Keywords: animals, bats, biodiversity, education, schor

Two participants with their bat boxes

11 kids came out to Schor Conservation Area on a snowy October Saturday to build bat boxes. Bats are in alarming decline due to decades of unwarranted human fear and persecution, white nose syndrome, and habitat loss. Building bat boxes provides our local bats with roosting habitats. Fun fact: bats are predators of night-flying insects and often eat half of their body weight in a single night.
