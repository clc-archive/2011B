I'm not sure what mapping in particular you're referencing, so I'll give you a list of various sources for wetland maps and habitat information. Please keep in mind the caveats I mentioned about the inaccuracies and incompleteness of various mapped resources. For a brief summary of which wetlands receive State or Federal protection, you can visit our web page, "Conserving Small Wetlands in the Hudson Valley": http://www.dec.ny.gov/lands/47486.html  As for habitat information, it's limited except in places that have been intensively studied by the NY Natural Heritage Program (http://www.dec.ny.gov/animals/29338.html) or in municipalities that have pursued Hudsonia's method of detailed habitat mapping (like in the Town of Ancram.)
 
Here are the on-line tools that have some wetland and habitat information:
 
Environmental Resource Mapper http://www.dec.ny.gov/animals/38801.html
Web-based, interactive map that includes locations of:
• Freshwater wetlands regulated by the State of New York (outside the Adirondack Park).
• New York's streams, rivers, lakes, and ponds; water quality classifications are also displayed.
• Animals and plants that are rare in New York, including those listed as Endangered or Threatened (generalized locations).
• Significant natural communities, such as rare or high-quality forests, wetlands, and other habitat types.
 
Conservation Guides http://guides.nynhp.org
Web-based animal, plant, and community guides include description, habitat, distribution, places to see, conservation issues, developed by the New York Natural Heritage Program. Once you’ve used the Environmental Resource Mapper to find which rare plants and animals or significant natural communities are near your land, use a conservation guide to learn more about them.
 
New York Nature Explorer: A Gateway to Biodiversity Information http://www.dec.ny.gov/animals/57844.html
New York Nature Explorer is a web-based tool for finding out about the animals, plants and habitats in your neighborhood or in your area of interest -- most of it at a county level. As a gateway to biodiversity information, New York Nature Explorer is intended for landowners, land managers, citizens, municipal officials, planners, consultants, project developers, researchers, students, and anyone else interested in the natural world.
 
USFWS Weltands Mapper
Web-based mapping platform that displays the National Wetland Inventory.
http://www.fws.gov/wetlands/Data/Mapper.html
