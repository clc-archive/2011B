Conserve a Local Farm (CALF)

Columbia County is historically an agriculturally-based community of proud local landowners and hardworking families who thrive off the natural resources and ecological vitality of the landscape. However, in recent years, this legacy and tradition of keeping farmlands intact for future generations has been threatened by sales of traditional farmland to non-farming buyers.

Through its Conserve a Local Farm (CALF) program, the Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) protects Columbia County farms by establishing conservation agreements that limit non-agricultural development on farmland. In order to do so, we identify tracts of farmable land that are currently for sale and at risk of being sold for development. We then work with conservation-minded farm buyers and direct them to our listings. We also partner with local realtors to promote the purchase of the land by individuals committed to farming it themselves or to partner with a farmer through CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program. It is our hope that the purchased land will be permanently protected with a voluntary conservation agreement that will preserve its ecological and conservation values, continue the tradition of farming in the county, and create financial benefits for all parties involved.

If you are interested in selling farmland and making sure it remains a working farm or are a prospective buyer seeking to become a farm owner, sign up for the CALF Program. CLC would be happy to discuss any questions you may have about a property, and hopes that by connecting buyers, sellers, and realtors, a successful match can be made. Contact Marissa Codey at Marissa@clctrust.org or 518.392.5252, ext. 211.
