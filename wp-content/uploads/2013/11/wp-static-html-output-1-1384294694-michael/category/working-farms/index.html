Partnership protects 1,265 acres of farmland

October 30, 2013 | Posted in Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: easements

The USDA-Natural Resources Conservation Service (NRCS), Scenic Hudson, Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC), Dutchess Land Conservancy (DLC), Dutchess County officials, Town of Red Hook officials, other local leaders, and farmers announced that federal Farm Bill funding has enabled Scenic Hudson and its partners to complete the purchase of conservation easements on seven working family farms in Dutchess and Columbia counties. CLC Collaborates with Dutchess Land Conservancy

August 5, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Farmer Landowner Match Program, Headline, Working Farms

The Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) is working with the Dutchess Land Conservancy (DLC) to expand its Farmer Landowner Match program into Dutchess County. The program, which previously focused solely on Columbia County, facilitates lease agreements between landowners seeking to have their land farmed and farmers seeking land.

High real estate costs make it difficult for farmers to purchase farmland and farm leasing is one strategy to help increase access to farmland. Leasing enables farmers to start and expand their operations with significantly less capital and risk.

Thanks to CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program I have been able to add significantly to my grazing acreage and thereby to continue to expand our cattle and sheep numbers. The two owners I work with are a great match for me because they were prepared to give long term leases and very much support having grazing animals on their land.

farmer Jerry Peele CONTINUE READING

Representative Gibson Praises CLC on House Floor

June 25, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: advocacy, Gibson

Representative Gibson and CLC staff visit farmer Rick Skoda at Triple Creek Farm in Taghkanic

Advocating for an amendment to the Farm Bill that would enable Land Trusts to take a more active role in conserving land, Representative Chris Gibson (R-NY) praised the Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) as a highly competent and passionate organization. He also stated that CLC, along with other regional land trusts, played a major role in preparing him for the Farm Bill debate.

Representative Chris Gibson has tremendous interest in conservation issues and we are grateful for his commitment to the future of Columbia County.
