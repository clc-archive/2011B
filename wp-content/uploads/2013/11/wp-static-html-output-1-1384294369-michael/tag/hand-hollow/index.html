Eastern Bluebird

October 1, 2013 | Posted in Headline | Keywords: birds, easements, hand hollow, pca, volunteer

Have you seen an Eastern bluebird this season? We have and they’re absolutely beautiful, which is one reason CLC is working hard to protect and create habitat for the state bird of NY. Named for its brilliant royal blue feathers, bluebirds nest in the cavities of decayed trees and stumps at the edges of grasslands where they feed on insects. Pond Returns

October 15, 2012 | Posted in Headline | Keywords: beaver, hand hollow, pca

In late August, we reported that one of the ponds at Hand Hollow Conservation Area had disappeared. A human-made concrete dam, built in the 1950’s, stopped functioning and the water drained. Neighbors expressed their regrets and the beavers listened. Beavers, active in the area, have plugged the breach and the pond has refilled. Beavers create ponds for protection using the waterways as safe passage between their home and food sources.

Pond No More

August 30, 2012 | Posted in CLC, Nature | Keywords: birds, hand hollow, pca, photo, video

We are committed to conserving our Public Conservation Areas, but that doesn’t mean that they will never change. At the Hand Hollow Conservation Area, the pond by the farm house near the Country Route 9 entrance has disappeared. Update: the pond has reappeared (10/15/2012). Built in the 1950’s, the human-made concrete dam is no longer functioning and the water has drained.

It’s now a lovely mud flat with a stream. Freshwater mussels dot the pond basin and small fish can be seen in the remaining pools. The shore line is dotted with native plants including spotted joe-pye weed, milkweed, and goldenrod. An American bittern, a member of the heron family, was recently seen foraging the upper edge of the mud flat (see video below). CONTINUE READING

Water Chestnut

June 28, 2012 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Nature | Keywords: fishing, hand hollow, invasive, pca, photos, volunteer

Thank you to all the volunteers who helped at the 5th Annual Water Chestnut Removal Days at the Hand Hollow Conservation Area! Working together we were able to clear a large portion of the southwestern portion of Meizinger Lake. This is the area that is most accessible to picnickers and anglers. The cleanup will allow for increased biodiversity and continued enjoyment of the lake. View the full photo album.
