Join Youth in Trail Building

August 5, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Trails | Keywords: pca, Round Ball Mountain

This summer, the Columbia Land Conservancy launched an exciting partnership with the Greenagers, a youth environmental organization based in the Berkshires that offers land stewardship training and summer employment opportunities. Since July 1, 2013, a seven person crew of young people ages 15 through 19 has been cutting a new trail as part of the expansion of CLC’s Round Ball Conservation Area in Ancram. The community is invited to join this dynamic, skilled, and hard-working team for a Community Trail Day on Sunday, August 11th, from 9-11am. HIKE FIVE!

August 5, 2013 | Posted in Headline

Congratulations to Susan Myers for completing the Hike Five! 2013 Summer Challenge. She visited and explored at least five of the destinations described in Columbia County Outdoors, and is recognized with a CLC award package, including CLC patch, water bottle, a keychain flashlight, and store coupon.

Susan reports that she explored Hand Hollow, Ooms, Schor, Borden Pond, and High Falls—some on several occasions–all of which happen to be CLC Public Conservation Areas. Though Susan has lived in Columbia County for nearly 20 years, this summer was the first time she visited High Falls, which she called the most spectacular of the sites. She also expressed a special fondness for the Schor Conservation Area – and Jon’s Pond – where she spends time with her children, grandchildren and dog. Even though she has already met the challenge, she has plans to visit Round Ball in early August.

The Hike Five! 2013 Summer Challenge is a program to encourage the community to get outdoors in Columbia County this summer. Participants can sign up by e-mailing tara@clctrust.org. Keep a list of the places you hike and visit, with dates, and photos too if you can. Let us know when you hike five! The challenge continues until Labor Day.

CLC Collaborates with Dutchess Land Conservancy

August 5, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Farmer Landowner Match Program, Headline, Working Farms

The Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) is working with the Dutchess Land Conservancy (DLC) to expand its Farmer Landowner Match program into Dutchess County. The program, which previously focused solely on Columbia County, facilitates lease agreements between landowners seeking to have their land farmed and farmers seeking land.

High real estate costs make it difficult for farmers to purchase farmland and farm leasing is one strategy to help increase access to farmland. Leasing enables farmers to start and expand their operations with significantly less capital and risk.

Thanks to CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program I have been able to add significantly to my grazing acreage and thereby to continue to expand our cattle and sheep numbers. The two owners I work with are a great match for me because they were prepared to give long term leases and very much support having grazing animals on their land.

Volunteers Improve Schor

July 17, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: pca, schor, volunteer

Volunteers recently joined Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) staff at the Schor Conservation Area for a productive trail day. At the summit, we thinned vegetation, revealing spectacular views. We also cleared out hanging vines and plants encroaching on the trails around Jon’s Pond. The Schor Conservation Area is one of 10 Public Conservation Areas that CLC manages. Volunteers provide crucial support for trail maintenance and site improvements.

Representative Gibson Praises CLC on House Floor

June 25, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline, Working Farms | Keywords: advocacy, Gibson

Representative Gibson and CLC staff visit farmer Rick Skoda at Triple Creek Farm in Taghkanic

Advocating for an amendment to the Farm Bill that would enable Land Trusts to take a more active role in conserving land, Representative Chris Gibson (R-NY) praised the Columbia Land Conservancy (CLC) as a highly competent and passionate organization. He also stated that CLC, along with other regional land trusts, played a major role in preparing him for the Farm Bill debate.

Representative Chris Gibson has tremendous interest in conservation issues and we are grateful for his commitment to the future of Columbia County.

Hike Five Summer Challenge

June 14, 2013 | Posted in CLC, Headline | Keywords: pca

Looking to spend more time outside this summer in beautiful Columbia County? Or perhaps you are already an avid hiker, walker, or outdoors explorer? Take part in our Hike Five! Summer Challenge. Between now and Labor Day, visit any five of the hikes, trails, or walks described in the guidebook Columbia County Outdoors (which includes CLC’s 10 Public Conservation Areas) and you’ll receive a Hike Five! package, including water bottle, stylish badge for your jacket or bag, and coupon for a discount in the CLC store.

So keep track of your tracks! Stop in to the store at 49 Main Street in Chatham and let us know where and when you hiked, e-mail a photo or two from each adventure, or drop a note in the comments. For more information, contact Tara Needham 518.392.5252, ext. 203 or tara@clctrust.org.
