Buying Farmland, Protecting Farmland

Seeking to buy farmland in Columbia County and become a part of the region’s proud agricultural legacy? The Conserve a Local Farm (CALF) Program, is eager to help you achieve your goal. In collaboration with local realtors, CLC identifies quality farmland in Columbia County that is currently for sale. We then promote the land to individuals committed to farming it themselves or establishing a business arrangement with a qualified farmer (potentially through CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program). CLC’s goal is to encourage the new landowners to establish a conservation agreement that will permanently protect the land from future development, thereby preserving the rural character of the landscape.

In collaboration with our local realtor community, CLC identifies properties throughout Columbia County that have the potential to be productively farmed for years to come. In order to take part in the CALF program there are several steps an interested buyer must take.

Steps to join our program

  • All buyers must complete the application form.
  • Once completed, buyers will be emailed a password, which will allow access to detailed listings of the properties, along with contact information for listing realtors. CLC staff will also contact all interested buyers to learn more about their goals, and to help them find an appropriate property. CLC values your privacy and will not pass on your information to real estate agents.
  • Once a property of interest is located on our site, prospective buyers should contact the real estate agent of record and mention that they were directed by CLC. The CALF program is not a fee-based activity and there are no fee arrangements with real estate agents.
  • At this stage, a conversation between buyer and CLC can commence regarding any mutual interest in establishing a conservation agreement and/or finding a farmer to cultivate the land through our Farmer Landowner Match Program.

Both the CALF Program and CLC’s Farmer Landowner Match Program can help buyers realizing their vision of purchasing important farmland and ensuring it stays in production. Our team of volunteer farmers can provide advice and assistance, and our Down to Earth educational workshop series helps all parties learn how to achieve long term, successful farming relationships.

Contact Marissa Codey at marissa@clctrust.org or 518.392,5252, ext. 211 for more information.
